# Privacy Policy for Bhagavad Gita Reader

**Last Updated: June 2026**

This Privacy Policy explains how the **Bhagavad Gita Reader** application handles your data. We believe in absolute privacy, transparency, and data ownership.

---

## 1. 100% Offline & Private
* **Zero Network Traffic**: The Bhagavad Gita Reader app is designed to run completely offline. The app **does not collect, store, or transmit** any personal identification, analytics, or behavioral data to remote servers.
* **Local Storage Only**: Your app preferences, bookmarks, and Spiritual Diary entries are saved locally on your device (using standard local web storage inside the secure Android WebView container). This data never leaves your device.

## 2. Permissions Used
The app requests a minimal set of native Android permissions to deliver core offline features:
* `RECEIVE_BOOT_COMPLETED`: Used to restore daily contemplation alarms and home screen widgets when you restart your phone.
* `POST_NOTIFICATIONS`: Used exclusively on Android 13+ to post local, scheduled quiet-hour meditation reminders.
* `WAKE_LOCK`: Used momentarily to let the system update the "Quote of the Day" home widget.

## 3. Third-Party Libraries & Ads
* **No Trackers**: This app is **100% tracker-free**. We do not use any commercial analytics packages (like Firebase Analytics, Mixpanel, or Flurry).
* **No Ads**: The application does not contain advertisements.
* **No Proprietary SDKs**: The app uses native Android system libraries and local open-source components, making it fully compliant with Free and Open Source Software (FOSS) standards.

---

## 4. Contact & Support
Since the app is fully open-source, you can review the entire source code, submit issues, or contribute features directly on our public repository.
